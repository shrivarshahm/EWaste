Core Problem

Without tracking, electronics disappear into informal and polluting disposal systems.

No chain of custody

Once discarded, devices often move through unknown handlers with no record of where toxic parts end up.

Unsafe worker conditions

Manual dismantling, acid stripping, and open burning expose workers to serious health risks.

Lost circular value

Repairable devices and recoverable metals are wasted instead of being brought back into productive use.

Lifecycle View

How the prototype manages electronics from disposal to final outcome.

1

Register

Every device or batch gets a digital record, quantity estimate, and hazard profile.

2

Assess

The system identifies items for reuse, batteries needing isolation, and high-risk waste loads.

3

Route

Batches are sent to formal refurbishers, dismantlers, or certified material recovery partners.

4

Measure

Outcomes are recorded as repaired, recovered, disposed safely, or still pending action.

Pollution Prevention

Hazard screening reduces contamination from batteries, circuit boards, and display waste.

Traceability

Every handoff is visible, which reduces informal leakage and improves accountability.

Resource Recovery

Repair, reuse, and material extraction become measurable outcomes instead of assumptions.
